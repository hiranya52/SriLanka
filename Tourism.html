Tourism

Sigiriya Rock Fortress

This ancient rock fortress and palace ruin is one of Sri Lanka's most iconic landmarks. The 200-meter-high rock features frescoes, mirror walls, and lion's paw entrance. A UNESCO World Heritage Site dating back to the 5th century.

Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic (Kandy)

Located in Kandy, this temple houses what is believed to be the tooth of Buddha. It's one of Buddhism's most sacred sites and a key pilgrimage destination. The annual Esala Perahera festival features a spectacular procession with decorated elephants.

Yala National Park

Sri Lanka's most famous wildlife park, known for its high density of leopards. Also home to elephants, sloth bears, crocodiles, and numerous bird species. The park combines scrub jungle with beaches and freshwater lakes.

Galle Fort

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this 17th-century Dutch fort is a living monument with colonial-era buildings, boutique hotels, restaurants, and shops within its walls. The fort offers stunning ocean views and a glimpse into Sri Lanka's colonial past.

Adam's Peak (Sri Pada)

Sacred to multiple religions, this 2,243m mountain features a footprint-shaped mark at its summit believed by Buddhists to be Buddha's, by Hindus to be Shiva's, and by Muslims and Christians to be Adam's. The sunrise pilgrimage climb is unforgettable.

Mirissa Beach

One of Sri Lanka's most beautiful beaches, known for its crescent-shaped bay, palm trees, and excellent whale watching opportunities (November-April). A perfect spot for relaxation, surfing, and enjoying fresh seafood.
